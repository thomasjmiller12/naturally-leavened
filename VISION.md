# Naturally Leavened — Project Vision & Ethos

## The Brand

**Naturally Leavened** is Haylee's sourdough brand — born from her background as a food scientist and her deep belief that sourdough doesn't need to be intimidating. It's four ingredients, a little patience, and a whole lot of love.

The name itself carries the philosophy: *naturally leavened* bread uses no commercial yeast. Just wild cultures, time, and care. It's the oldest form of bread-making in the world, and it's simpler than most people think.

## The Mission

Haylee started selling sourdough to friends and neighbors. Then they started asking *how* she made it. Naturally Leavened grew from those kitchen-table conversations into something bigger — a brand centered on **teaching, sharing, and making sourdough accessible to everyone**.

Her core message: **Sourdough is easier than you think.** You don't need expensive equipment or a culinary degree. You need flour, water, salt, a starter, and someone to show you the way.

## Who Haylee Is

- **Food scientist** — she understands fermentation, gluten networks, and the Maillard reaction at a molecular level
- **Teacher at heart** — she gets excited about breaking down the "why" behind every step, without the jargon
- **Community builder** — bread is meant to be shared, and so is the knowledge of how to make it
- **Not pretentious** — this isn't about artisan gatekeeping. It's about a friend showing you how to bake your first loaf

## The Website's Purpose

This site serves three goals:

1. **Inspire** — When someone lands on this site, they should feel warmth, curiosity, and the thought: *"I could do this."* The scroll-through bread journey, the warm photography, the gentle animations — everything should feel like stepping into a cozy kitchen.

2. **Educate** — The recipes page, the sourdough calculator, and the class curriculum are all real, useful tools. Haylee's food science background means every recipe comes with the *why*, not just the *how*.

3. **Connect** — The classes are the heart of the business. Small groups, hands-on learning, personal attention. The site should make it easy and inviting to sign up (text to reserve a spot).

## Design Philosophy

### Warm & Inviting
The color palette is drawn from bread itself — warm creams, golden crusts, earthy browns, sage greens. Nothing cold, nothing clinical. Every page should feel like it smells like fresh bread.

### Animated & Alive
The site should feel like a living thing. Floating flour particles, smooth scroll transitions, entrance animations, the bread journey that unfolds as you scroll. Not flashy for the sake of it — animated because bread-making is a *process*, and the website should honor that sense of transformation.

### Simple & Approachable
Just like Haylee's approach to sourdough, the design should never feel overwhelming. Clean typography, generous whitespace, clear navigation. The elegance comes from restraint, not complexity.

### Real Photography
Haylee's own photos from her Instagram and kitchen are the visual backbone. They're real, warm, and personal — not sterile stock photos. This authenticity is a strength.

## Content Tone

- **First person** — Haylee speaks directly to the reader
- **Encouraging** — "You can do this" energy throughout
- **Knowledgeable but not academic** — science explained in plain English
- **Warm humor** — bread puns welcome, pretension not
- **Honest** — sourdough takes time, and that's okay. We don't sugarcoat the process
